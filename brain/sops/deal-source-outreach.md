# SOP: Deal Source Outreach

**File:** deal-source-outreach.md
**Category:** Sales
**Status:** 🔴 Draft
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-05

---

## Purpose

Generate consistent deal flow through operator and network relationships. Position Liberty Equity Xchange as the capital desk behind multiple operators so deals come inbound rather than requiring constant direct outreach.

---

## Trigger

- Weekly pipeline review shows fewer than 5 active deals
- New contact identified as a potential deal source
- Attending an investor event or community

---

## Steps

### Step 1 — Identify Target Deal Sources

Prioritize contacts who:
- Work with multiple investors
- See deals before financing is secured
- Need fast funding solutions
- Would benefit from referral compensation

**Target categories:**

| Category | Where to Find Them |
|----------|--------------------|
| Wholesalers | SubTo community, Facebook investor groups, local REIA |
| Real estate agents | Investor-focused agents, listing agents on distressed properties |
| Operators / flippers | Masterminds, SubTo, deal discussions |
| Brokers | Mortgage brokers who can't fund investor deals in-house |
| Developers | Local investor networks, LinkedIn |

### Step 2 — Send Introduction

Keep it short and direct. Lead with value, not a pitch.

**Text / DM example:**
> "Hey [Name] — I specialize in helping investors secure funding for their deals. Fix and flip, DSCR, bridge, construction — if you come across projects that need capital, feel free to send them my way. Happy to take a look and let you know what we can do."

**Email example:**
> Subject: Capital solutions for your investor deals
>
> [Name],
>
> I work with real estate investors across the country to structure and place capital for deals — fix and flip, DSCR rentals, bridge loans, and construction financing.
>
> If you're working with investors who need funding, I'd be happy to take a look at what they have. I can usually give a quick read on whether we can make it work.
>
> Alex Angus
> Liberty Equity Xchange
> libertyequityx.com

### Step 3 — Qualify the Relationship

When a deal source responds, ask:
- How many deals do you typically see per month?
- What types of deals — fix and flip, rentals, new construction?
- What markets are you most active in?
- Are your investors typically looking for fast closes?

### Step 4 — Set Expectations

Explain how the relationship works:
- They send deals → Liberty Equity Xchange evaluates and structures capital → deal closes → referral compensation paid on funded deals
- All lender relationships route through Liberty Equity Xchange
- Response time: quick read within 24–48 hours on every submission

### Step 5 — Record the Deal Source

Add to `brain/deals/deal-sources.md` and create contact file in `brain/people/` using `_TEMPLATE.md`.

Tag: `deal_source`

### Step 6 — Follow Up Regularly

A deal source that goes quiet needs a periodic check-in.

**Re-engagement message:**
> "Hey [Name] — just checking in. Anything in your pipeline that needs capital? Happy to take a look."

---

## AI Instructions

- When deal flow is slow, suggest outreach to existing deal sources before prospecting new ones
- Track which deal sources are sending deals consistently vs. going quiet
- Prioritize building relationships with sources who work with multiple investors (wholesalers, brokers) over one-off referrals
- Every new deal source should be added to `brain/deals/deal-sources.md`
