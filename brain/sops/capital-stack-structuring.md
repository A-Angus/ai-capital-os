# SOP: Capital Stack Structuring

**File:** capital-stack-structuring.md
**Category:** Operations
**Status:** 🔴 Draft
**Last Updated:** 2026-03-05

---

## Purpose

Build the optimal capital stack for a deal — senior debt, gap capital, mezzanine, or full stack — so every dollar of funding is accounted for and the deal can close.

---

## Trigger

Deal has been scored A or B and lender matching has identified a gap or a multi-layer structure is needed.

---

## Capital Stack Layers

| Layer | Description | Sources |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Senior debt | Primary loan — first position | Institutional lenders, asset-based lenders |
| Bridge / gap capital | Fills gap between senior loan and total need | Carrol Walton Grizzle, Eric Fuller, Federal Finance |
| Mezzanine | Junior debt above senior, below equity | Edgewood Capital Advisors, Farragut Capital Partners |
| Transactional | Short-term bridge for closing only | Mikey |
| Equity / borrower contribution | Borrower's skin in the game | Borrower funds |

---

## Steps

### Step 1 — Define Total Capital Need

```
Total Capital Need = Purchase Price + Rehab Budget + Closing Costs + Reserves
```

### Step 2 — Determine Senior Loan Amount

Based on lender's LTV or ARV parameters:

```
Fix and flip:   Senior loan = ARV × 65–75%
DSCR rental:    Senior loan = Purchase price × 70–80%
Bridge:         Senior loan = Current value × 65–75%
```

### Step 3 — Identify the Gap

```
Gap = Total Capital Need – Senior Loan Amount
```

If gap > 0, determine the best source to fill it.

### Step 4 — Fill the Gap

| Gap Size | Strategy |
|----------|---------|
| Small gap (<10% of deal) | Borrower equity or gap capital |
| Medium gap (10–30%) | Private capital partner or mezz |
| Large gap (>30%) | 100% financing structure (Eric Fuller if in NC, WV, OH, GA) or deal needs repricing |

### Step 5 — Present the Full Stack

Show the borrower exactly how the deal is funded.

**Example presentation:**
```
Deal: 9318 Sherbourne St, Houston TX
─────────────────────────────────────
Purchase price:         $180,000
Rehab budget:            $51,000
Total project cost:     $231,000

ARV:                    $340,000

Senior loan (68% ARV):  $231,000   ← Rehab Financial Group
Gap:                         $0    ← Fully covered by senior

Borrower equity needed:      $0 (closing costs only)
─────────────────────────────────────
Structure: Fix and flip — single lender, full coverage
```

### Step 6 — Confirm Borrower Can Execute

Before submitting to lenders, verify:
- [ ] Borrower can cover closing costs and reserves
- [ ] Borrower agrees to the structure and costs
- [ ] Timeline is realistic
- [ ] Exit strategy is solid

---

## AI Instructions

- Always show the full math when structuring a deal — never leave numbers vague
- If a single lender covers the full stack, say so clearly
- If multiple layers are needed, identify each source explicitly
- Never over-promise on leverage — use conservative lender parameters
- If the deal can't be structured to make sense for the borrower, say so directly
